Greek Spinach Rice

With tons of fresh herb flavor,. this Greek spinach rice is a simple dish with complex flavors. Serve as a side dish with grilled chicken, lamb, or stew, or by itself as a vegetarian main course. The fresh squeeze of lemon juice and feta crumble are critical to the taste, don't leave them out!

Ingredients

  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 1 1/2 cups medium-grain white rice
  • 2 teaspoon large-grain kosher salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
  • 1 pinch cayenne pepper, or to taste
  • 1 bunch green onions. thinly sliced
  • 1/2 sup packed finely chopped fresh dill
  • 1/2 cup packed finely chopped fresh mint leaves
  • 1 pound frozen chopped spinach, thawed and squeezed dry
  • 2 3/4 cups cold water, or more as needed
  • kosher salt to taste

To Serve

  • 3 medium lemons, cut in half
  • 3/4 cup crumbled feta cheese
  • 2 tablespoons pickled red onions or to taste (optional)
  • 1 pinch how chili flakes, or to taste (optional)
  • 1 drizzle olive oil

Directions

  1. Heat oil in a saucepan over medium-high heat
  2. Add rice and stir until every grain is coated in oil and you hear it start to sizzle.
  3. Stir in 2 tablespoons salt, pepper, cayenne, and green onions and cook for about 1 minute
  4. Add dill, mint spinach and 2 3/4 cups water. Mix until thoroughly combined and bring to a simmer
  5. Cover, reduce heat to medium-low, and cook until rice is tender, about 25 minutes. If rice is not quite ready at 25 minutes, add a splash of water, cover, and continue to cook until it is ready.
  6. Uncover and stir well. Cover again and let sit for 5 minutes. Taste and adjust salt if necessary.
  7. Divide rice mixture evenly into six bowls. Squeeze 1/2 lemon over each serving, then top with feta cheese, pickled onions, chili flakes, and a drizzle of olive oil.
